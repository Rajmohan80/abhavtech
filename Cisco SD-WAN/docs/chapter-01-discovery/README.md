# Discovery & Assessment

This chapter provides comprehensive discovery and assessment procedures for planning an SD-WAN deployment. It covers infrastructure inventory, traffic analysis, application requirements, and readiness assessment.

## Chapter Overview

The discovery phase is critical for understanding your current WAN infrastructure and requirements before migrating to Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN. This chapter guides you through systematic assessment of:

- Current WAN infrastructure and device inventory
- Traffic patterns and application requirements
- Transport options and security requirements
- Business requirements and success criteria
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategies

## Sections in This Chapter

- **1.1 Current WAN Infrastructure Inventory** - Document existing WAN devices, circuits, and configurations
- **1.2 WAN Traffic Analysis** - Analyze traffic patterns, bandwidth utilization, and application flows
- **1.3 Application Requirements** - Identify critical applications and their network requirements
- **1.4 Transport Assessment** - Evaluate MPLS, Internet, and other transport options
- **1.5 Security Requirements** - Define security policies and compliance requirements
- **1.6 SD-WAN Readiness Assessment** - Assess organizational readiness for SD-WAN migration
- **1.7 Business Requirements** - Document business drivers and objectives
- **1.8 Success Criteria & KPIs** - Define measurable success criteria and KPIs
- **1.9 Risk Assessment** - Identify and mitigate migration risks

## How to Use This Chapter

1. **Start with Infrastructure Inventory (1.1)** to document your current state
2. **Analyze traffic patterns (1.2)** to understand bandwidth and application needs
3. **Define requirements (1.3-1.5)** for applications, transport, and security
4. **Assess readiness (1.6-1.7)** to ensure organizational and technical preparedness
5. **Establish metrics (1.8-1.9)** for measuring success and managing risks

Each section includes templates, checklists, and examples specific to the Abhavtech deployment scenario.
